The process begins with the company determining the contact details of potential customers. After this, there are three possible paths the company can take to gather these details. 

First, the company might decide to purchase contact details specifically for making cold calls. Alternatively, marketing staff could participate in exhibitions to gather contact information. Lastly, the company might already know a person who is interested in their product. Regardless of the path chosen, the process converges to the next step.

Once the contact details are obtained, call center staff reach out to the customer. During this call, they identify the appropriate contact person, determine the customer's budget, and assess their level of interest in the product.

Following the assessment of customer interest, there are two main paths the process can take. The first path involves making an online presentation. This presentation can be conducted either by a sales representative or a pre-sales employee. After the presentation, the company waits for a response from the customer.

While waiting, the company has two options for follow-up. The first option involves a sales representative calling the customer. This call can lead to further actions based on the customer's response. The second option is to continue waiting without immediate follow-up. Eventually, both options lead to the company creating a quotation for the customer.

The second main path after assessing customer interest involves the head of development acquiring the customer. Similar to the first path, an online presentation is made, either by a sales representative or a pre-sales employee. After the presentation, the company waits for the customer's response.

During this waiting period, the company again has two options for follow-up. The first option involves a sales representative calling the customer, which can lead to further actions based on the customer's response. The second option is to continue waiting without immediate follow-up. Eventually, both options lead to the company creating a quotation for the customer.

Finally, after the quotation is created, the process concludes.
