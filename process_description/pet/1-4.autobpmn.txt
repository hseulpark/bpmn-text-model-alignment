The process begins when a company decides to go public. Following this decision, the company selects underwriters to manage the public offering. The underwriters then prepare a registration statement, which needs to be approved by the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). After preparing the registration statement, the underwriters check to ensure that the issue complies with all necessary regulations.

At this point, two activities occur simultaneously. On one hand, the underwriters start to determine the issue price of the stock. As part of this process, they arrange a road show to present the offering to potential investors. On the other hand, the underwriters wait for the approval of the registration statement from the SEC.

Once both the road show is arranged and the registration statement is approved, the process continues with the underwriters receiving clearance from the SEC. With this clearance, the underwriters are able to finalize the issue price of the stock. They then enter into a firm commitment to purchase the stock from the company.

Finally, the underwriters offer the stock to the public, marking the completion of the process.
