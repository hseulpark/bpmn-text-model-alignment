The process begins with the Coordination Unit finalizing the dates for an upcoming meeting. Once the dates are set, the Support Officer updates all group calendars to reflect the new meeting schedule. Following this, the Support Officer creates meeting folders to organize the necessary materials for the meeting.

Next, the Support Officer ensures that all appropriate documents are uploaded to the system, making them accessible for the meeting participants. After the documents are uploaded, the Support Officer advises the Committee Members to read all the documents in preparation for the meeting.

The Committee Members then hold the meeting as scheduled. After the meeting concludes, the Support Office is responsible for producing the minutes, which are the official record of what was discussed and decided during the meeting.

Once the minutes are produced, the Coordination Unit conducts a quality assurance (QA) check on them to ensure accuracy and completeness. After the QA check is completed, the Coordination Unit sends the reviewed minutes to all Committee Members for their records.

Finally, the Support Officer updates the departmental records to reflect the outcomes and decisions made during the meeting. This marks the end of the process.
