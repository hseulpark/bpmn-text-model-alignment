The process begins with the Coordination Unit drafting a schedule of meetings. Once the draft schedule is prepared, the Coordination Unit adds these draft dates to all relevant calendars. After this, the Support Officer reviews the dates to ensure they are appropriate. If the Support Officer identifies any issues, they suggest modifications to the schedule.

Following the suggestions, the Coordination Unit rechecks all the dates to incorporate the proposed changes. They then look for any potential conflicts that might arise with the revised schedule. Once the Coordination Unit is satisfied that the schedule is free of conflicts, they send the final version to the Committee Members.

Upon receiving the final schedule, the Committee Members check their diaries to confirm their availability. If any Committee Members find conflicts with the proposed dates, they inform the Coordination Unit of these issues. The process concludes after the Committee Members have communicated any scheduling conflicts to the Coordination Unit.
