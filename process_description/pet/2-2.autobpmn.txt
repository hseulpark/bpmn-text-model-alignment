The process begins when a customer sends their data to the customer service department. Once the data is received, the customer service team enters this information into the CRM system. They then compare the newly entered data with the existing internal customer data. Following this, the customer service team checks the data for completeness and plausibility.

At this point, a decision is made. If the data is found to be incomplete or implausible, the customer service team corrects the data. Once the data is corrected, or if it was already complete and plausible, the process continues.

Next, another decision is made. If a new customer object is needed, the customer service team creates it and prepares the contract documents for the customer's power supplier switch. If a new customer object is not needed, the process moves forward without this step.

The contract documents are then checked by the CIS (Customer Information System). If issues are found with the documents, the customer service team analyzes and resolves these issues, then generates new contract documents. If no issues are found, the process continues without these additional steps.

Once the contract documents are finalized, the CIS sends a confirmation document to the customer and also sends a request to the grid operator. The grid operator checks the request to ensure it aligns with supplier agreements. After this check, the grid operator sends a response.

Another decision is made here. If there is a conflict, the grid operator informs all suppliers and demands resolution. If there is no conflict, the process continues.

The grid operator then communicates with the old supplier and terminates the sales agreement. Following this, the customer service team creates a CIS contract, which the customer reviews. The customer then decides whether to confirm or withdraw from the switch contract.

If the customer confirms the contract, several actions occur simultaneously. The grid operator transmits the power meter data, computes the final billing, and sends this billing to the old supplier. Meanwhile, the old supplier creates the final billing and sends it to the customer. Once these tasks are completed, the customer service team receives the meter data and imports it into their systems.

If the customer withdraws from the contract, the process skips these steps and moves towards completion.

Finally, the process concludes, marking the end of the sequence of activities.
