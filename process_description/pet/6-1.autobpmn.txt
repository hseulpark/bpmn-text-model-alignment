The process begins when ACME AG receives invoices. Once the invoices are received, the secretariat performs a visual inspection of each invoice. After this inspection, the secretariat forwards the invoice to an accounting employee.

The accounting employee then identifies the suppliers who are charging for the invoice. Following this, the employee creates a new invoice and checks the items listed on it. The employee also notes the cost center and the managers responsible for it on a document referred to as a docket. This docket, along with a copy of the invoice, is sent to the internal mail system.

Next, the accounting employee sends the docket and the invoice copy to the cost center manager. The process then checks if the cost center manager has received the invoice copy. If the manager has received it, they review the content for accuracy.

If the content is accurate, the cost center manager notes their approval code on the docket and returns the invoice copy. If there are multiple cost center managers involved, the invoice copy is passed to the next manager. If the cost center manager rejects the approval, they send the invoice back to the accounting department for clarification with the vendor.

In cases where clarification is needed, the accounting employee consults with the cost center managers. After resolving any issues, the accounting employee sends the invoice copy to the cost center managers again and forwards it to the commercial manager.

The commercial manager conducts a commercial audit and issues approval for payment. The process then involves the board checking the invoice. If the board approves, the commercial manager moves the invoice copy and docket back to the accounting employee.

If there are any complaints, the accounting employee resolves them. Once resolved, the employee gives payment instructions and closes the invoice. The process concludes when the invoice is closed, and the cycle can repeat if necessary.
