The process begins when a customer request is received. Once the request is received, it is checked to determine the next steps. After checking the request, a decision is made. If the decision leads to a registration process, there are two possible paths: manual registration or automatic registration. 

If manual registration is chosen, it is completed before moving to the next decision point. Similarly, if automatic registration is selected, it is completed before reaching the same decision point. After either type of registration, the process continues to another decision point.

At this decision point, if the condition is met, the process involves searching for an attraction provider. Once the search is completed, another decision is made. If the decision is to proceed, the next step is to book the attraction. After attempting to book the attraction, there are two possible outcomes: receiving a rejection or receiving a booking confirmation.

If a rejection is received, the process continues to the next decision point. If a booking confirmation is received, a confirmation is sent to the customer, and then the process moves to the same decision point. Regardless of whether a rejection or confirmation is received, the process eventually returns to the previous decision point.

If the initial decision after checking the customer request does not lead to registration, a notification is sent to the customer. After sending the notification, the process reaches the final decision point, which leads to the end of the process.
