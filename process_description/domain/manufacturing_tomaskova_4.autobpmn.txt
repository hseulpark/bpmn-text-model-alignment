The process begins with an initial step that sets everything in motion. Immediately, a decision point is encountered where a choice is made based on a specific condition. If the condition is true, the process loops back to this decision point, effectively creating a cycle until the condition is no longer met.

Once the condition is resolved, the process moves forward to the first main activity, which involves planning the production. After the production planning is complete, the next step is to assign workers to their respective tasks. Following the assignment of workers, the process continues with designing the production, ensuring that all necessary designs are in place for the production to proceed smoothly.

After the production design is finalized, the process advances to delivering the product. Once the product is delivered, the next step involves logging out of the production phase, marking the completion of the production activities. Following the production logout, a quality control check is conducted to ensure that the product meets the required standards.

After the quality control is completed, another decision point is reached. Here, the process can take one of two paths. If the product is ready to be finished, it is registered to the finished stock. Once registered, it is then moved to the finished goods stock, concluding this path.

Alternatively, if the product is not yet finished, it is registered to the semi-finished stock. After registration, it is loaded into the semi-finished stock, completing this alternative path.

Finally, regardless of the path taken, the process concludes with an end step, marking the completion of the entire process.
